FAQ

Who’s running the program?

Work for America is a partnership between Bay Area-based social enterprise MobileWorks, Inc. and a consortium of civic job training programs and nonprofit organizations. MobileWorks to provide technology skills training through microwork. We’re able to offer participating companies an exceptional value (over 50% off the normal MobileWorks pricing) while guaranteeing the quality of work.

Who’s eligible to sign up?

Any business or individual worldwide permitted to do business with the USA can reserve a project through the Work for America program.

Do I get anything else for signing up?

You’ll be listed as among the organizations supporting the program.

When will the work begin?

Work will begin January 31st, 2013 and be carried out through February and March 2013.

How is the Work for America program able to offer good work at such low prices?

These work opportunities are being offered as part of job training programs, so trainees have the opportunity to practice and develop useful skills in a real-world context as part of doing the work. The program costs are subsidized.

Who’s doing the work?

Workers are selected from US-based job training programs in urban areas. They are supervised by trained technology instructional staff in carrying out the five types of projects offered.

How do I know the work will be done correctly?

Participants that are completing the work tasks are working with trained technology instructors who are supervising the work. All the work will be checked by hand by professional instructors and managerial staff.

When do I pay?

You won’t be billed anything until work actually begins. If you don’t like the quality of the work, you won’t pay anything.

Why is this offer limited to a certain number of projects?

We only have a certain number of trainees available. If the offer is successful, we’ll offer it again in 2013 with additional trainees.

What’s the process once I sign up for the program?

A project manager from Work for America will contact you by your preferred method (email or telephone) within 1 business day of your order to discuss your project requirements in depth. You’ll receive a preliminary statement of work that covers the nature of work that you’ll be carrying out, the format of the results you’ll receive, and the timeframe for results. Then it’ll be assigned to a team at one of our partner job training centers in the United States to be completed. You’ll be able to monitor the progress of the project as it’s carried out, and the team will stay in close touch with you to ensure the project’s success.
